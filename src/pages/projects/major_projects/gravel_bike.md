<p style="text-align: center;">This time I machined the flat mount brake interface and the dropout as a single piece of stainless. OP10 involved cutting dovetails into the stock for the vice and removing some extra material.</p>

<div class="markdown_img_container">
<img class="markdown_image" src="/major_projects/gravel_bike/dropout_op20.webp">
<img class="markdown_image" src="/major_projects/gravel_bike/dropout.webp">
</div>

<p style="text-align: center;">The finished dropout. All together it took 2 full days of machining.</p>

<div class="markdown_img_container">
<img class="markdown_image" src="/major_projects/gravel_bike/chainstays.webp">
<img class="markdown_image" src="/major_projects/gravel_bike/rear.webp">
</div>

<p style="text-align: center;">I started welding by tacking the chainstay assembly. The aluminum foil is to shield the backsides of the dropout welds to prevent any sugaring. I also flowed argon inside the tubes through the bottom bracket to prevent sugaring on the inside of the 3d printed yoke welds. Once the chainstays were tacked, I tacked the seat stays and welded the rear triangle.</p>

<div class="markdown_img_container">
<img class="markdown_image" src="/major_projects/gravel_bike/ovalized_yoke.webp">
</div>

<p style="text-align: center;">After fully welding the rear triangle I realized I had ovalized the front of the seat stay yoke from the weld distortion. I should have waited to weld the yoke to the seat tube until after I had tacked the top tube in place. I ended up filing the top/bottom of the yoke until the top tube fit.</p>

<div class="markdown_img_container">
<img class="markdown_image" src="/major_projects/gravel_bike/frame.webp">

<p style="text-align: center;">Welding the front triangle ended up being pretty straightforward.</p>
