<h4 style="text-align: center;">Each year the machine shop hosts a secret santa exchange at the Christmas party. Everyone has to make their on gift in the machine shops, and this year I decided to make an aluminum rubik's cube. The idea and design was taken from <a class="accent" href="https://lulabs.net/machining/ss-cube/">https://lulabs.net/machining/ss-cube/</a>. I was on a tight timeline (and busy with school) so I decided to make the project out of aluminum instead of stainless, and I took advantage of CNC machining to speed everything up. The center, edge, and corner pieces were cnc machined, and the core pieces (core and stems) were manually machined. The project ended up taking around 5-6 days of work.</h4>

<h4 style="text-align: center;">I started with the center pieces, which are simple 2 op mill parts. The inside gets radii for the sliding surfaces as well as a threaded bore for the core pieces. I was a bit too ambitious and tried to machine the second op of two center pieces at once. Because of jaw tilt, only one part was actually clamped, and the second part went flying as soon as I broke through the remaining stock. Oops.</h4>

<h4 style="text-align: center;">Next was manually machining the core and stems. The stems hold a captive bolt and spring which preloads all the pieces together and takes up any slop. The core was interesting to machine. On the mill, I made a cube and drilled/tapped all 6 threaded holes. Using a 4-jaw chuck on the lathe, I turned down the 6 sides. To save time setting up the 4-jaw chuck each time, I indicated off a bolt threaded into the holes. Not ideal, but it was quick and the lathe ops were only for clearance.</h4>

<h4 style="text-align: center;">Last up was engraving the patterns onto the sides. I considered engraving all 54 faces on the CNC, but quickly realized it would take too long. Instead, I found a metal marking spray for aluminum which can be engraved with a CO2 laser. The alignment wasn't perfect, and one side has extra markings where I had to go over the design twice (forgot to set the laser power correctly), but it worked out pretty well in the end. Super happy with how it came out!</h4>
