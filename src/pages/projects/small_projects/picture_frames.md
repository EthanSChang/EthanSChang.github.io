At the end of the 2025 spring quarter, my friend asked me to make billet picture frames as gifts for my boss at the Cal Poly machine shops. He made the design, and I was responsible for machining the parts. 

<div class="markdown_img_container">
<img class="markdown_image" src="/small_projects/picture_frame/setup.webp">
</div>

The setup in the machine. I put a sacrifical piece of stock underneath the main piece of stock to allow me cut the whole height of the part without hitting the table. I marked the approximate part size on the stock so I didn't crash into one of the clamps. Since I had to offset the stock to use the 2 t-slots, I only had ~1/2" of y-axis travel clearance in this op.
